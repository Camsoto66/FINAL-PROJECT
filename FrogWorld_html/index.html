About Frogs

Frogs are amphibians, which means they can live both in water and on land. They are known for their jumping abilities, sticky tongues, and loud croaks.

Why Frogs Are Important

Frogs play a major role in ecosystems around the world. They help control insect populations, serve as food for other animals, and act as environmental indicators. Because frogs have sensitive skin, changes in water quality, pollution, or climate often affect them first.

Scientists study frog populations to understand the health of wetlands and forests. When frogs disappear, it often signals that an ecosystem is under stress.

Frogs and the Environment

Many frog species are declining due to habitat loss, pollution, disease, and climate change. Protecting wetlands and reducing chemical use helps frogs survive and keeps ecosystems balanced.

  • Protect local wetlands
  • Reduce pesticide use
  • Keep water sources clean
  • Support conservation efforts
