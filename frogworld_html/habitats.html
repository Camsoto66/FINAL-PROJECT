Common Frog Habitats

Ponds and Lakes

Many frogs live near freshwater like ponds, lakes, and wetlands. These areas provide water for laying eggs and plenty of insects to eat.

Rainforests

Tropical rainforests are home to some of the most colorful and unique frogs. These environments are warm, humid, and full of plants for climbing and hiding.

Forests and Grasslands

Some frogs live on land in forests or grassy areas, staying near moisture and returning to water when it’s time to breed.

Underground and Desert Areas

A few special frogs can survive in dry environments by burrowing underground and staying dormant until rain arrives.

Why Habitat Matters

Frogs need clean water and healthy environments to survive. Pollution, habitat loss, and climate change are major threats to frog populations worldwide.
